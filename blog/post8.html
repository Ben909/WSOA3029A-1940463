Group discussion

Throughout the second semester, our WhatsApp group has revealed many interesting conversations regarding information and how visualizations communicate theories or key areas of study. One of these readings suggests that data visualization is a matter of life and death. Alberto Cairo reasons the modern use of data visualization is to expand knowledge to wider audiences and that analyzing data firstly means we have to understand why it is important or relevant to collect certain types of data. His examples showcase how century old visualizations have consequences for contemporary graphic designers. A popular diagram of the causes of mortality during wars in the east can reveal to us the narratives needed to counteract similar circumstances in contemporary society.

If we are to understand the stories behind graphs, we must urge readers to become actively involved in the narratives that unfold when we provide insightful discoveries about certain topics. For example, the relationship between healthcare and the practice of social distancing during the Covid-19 pandemic. Once readers realize the importance of protective measures by seeing specific timelines infront of them, they will recognize why these social barriers are so important. Without appreciating the effectiveness of social distancing, it becomes difficult to comprehend the narrative of life and death which we are surround by in present times.
